  1. 6 Weeks

    January 16th

    Your baby is starting to form and shape its body. The earliest genetic test that is available to you and your baby is the first Ultrasound:


    First Trimester Ultrasound


    The first trimester ultrasound is not a diagonistic test, but rather a screening test. It can tell if your baby is at risk for carrying a genetic disorder. The risk for false positives and false negatives is very high.

  2. 10 Weeks

    February 28th

    At 10 weeks, you have the option to perform your first genetic tests. these tests include:


    CVS


    Usually performed between the 10th and 12th weeks of pregnancy, CVS is a procedure which obtains a small sample of the placenta. The sample is then tested and can help determine nearly all chromosomal abnormalities.


    cfDNA


    Performed as early as the 10th week of pregnancy, the cfDNA test takes a sample of the mother's blood which contains a small percent of the baby's dna. This is then tested for Trisomy 21, a chromosomal abnormality often referred to as "Down Syndrome".

  3. 15 Weeks

    April 1st

    At 15 weeks, your baby has now grown enough for you to have the option of the following tests:


    Amniocentesis


    Sometimes reffered to a as the "amniotic fluid test", this is a procedure in which your doctor will extract a small amount of the amniotic fluid surrounding your baby, to test for chromosome disorders such as "down syndrome". It can also be used to determine the sex of the baby.


    Multiple Marker Screening


    This screening involves drawing a small amount of the mother's blood which contains various substances from the baby. The sample is then examined to determine if birth defects such as Spina Bifida and Down Syndrome are present
